Maze of Ith

I’m not going to review all those weird lands that don’t make mana, like Safe Haven and Sorrow’s Path, but I’ll make an exception for Maze of Ith, which is by far the best. Try to think of this more like a Rock Jockey than a land (we all remember that card, right?) but obviously it’s tutorable with Expedition Map and stuff.

I originally didn’t even have a review for this card, since until 2016 it had never been reprinted and it was a bit pricey, plus it had that old old land frame that just never looked right, along with its gross art. Normally I love weird, old cards, but it was also too popular and I’ve always been a bit of a hipster. Now I feel like it’s almost hidden tech again.

Fun fact: In an old issue of InQuest magazine, they had a quiz to test your Magic knowledge, and the final question was this: You have no permanents in play, no cards in your hand, and you’re at 1 life. Your opponent plays a Force of Nature. What is the only card you could draw on your next turn to stabilize your position?

Even funner fact: I don’t think the answer has changed since then!

Okay, almost none of that was an actual review. I don’t know, it’s a great card. Play it. But I feel like I’m giving non-land advice at this point.

Urza’s Workshop

Okay, what the frick is this? Let’s get this out of the way: “Urza’s” should never have been a land type! The Urzatron should have been name-based, not type-based, and as far as I know, the only reason they created the types was to make the original text work as written, but that’s just not a very good reason, especially when the intent was clearly to reference the name... land types didn’t even really exist when the Urzatron was printed! And since types have to be a single word, “Urza’s” became a type in itself, which really feels like some kind of bug in the game (don’t get me started on how they had to hyphenate “power-plant”). Sure, we get Urza’s Saga poking fun at that, but then this card comes along and tries to pretend like it was all intentional! And fine, sometimes Magic cards can be funny like that, but it still feels wrong. Now, onto my actual review: this card is good. I’m personally not a big fan of the Urzatron in colorless commander, but this makes it look fairly tempting. Urza’s Factory counts too, plus Urza’s Saga, of course (but that one doesn’t stick around too long). And since Metalcraft is almost always on, this will be producing at least two mana fairly regularly. Of course, it does force you to play the Urzatron at least and probably Urza’s Factory too, which aren’t going to do that much by themselves. I can’t say for certain whether it’s worth it, but I like cards that make you commit to the bit.
